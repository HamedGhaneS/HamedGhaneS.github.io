About Me

At the intersection of mathematics, control systems, and neuroscience, I bring a unique perspective to artificial intelligence and human cognition. After a decade as an Assistant Professor in Control Engineering, my fascination with the human brain led me to transition into neuroscience, where I now conduct postdoctoral research at the University of Glasgow.

Mathematical Foundation

My expertise in linear and nonlinear algebra provides deep insights into the mathematical structures powering modern AI and large language models. This foundation enables me to approach both artificial and biological intelligence from a rigorous analytical perspective.

Systems Expertise

Years of research in control engineering have given me mastery in system modeling and analysis, skills that are crucial for understanding complex neural networks and developing advanced AI systems.
